Bibimbap

Description

A traditional Korean fried rice with mixed vegetables recipe, known as Bimbap. That features native ingredients and characteristics of sweetness, savoriness, and spiciness.

Ingredients

  • One English cucumber, cut into matchsticks
  • One-fourth gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
  • One bunch of fresh spinach, cut into thin strips
  • One tablespoon of soy sauce
  • Two teaspoons of olive oil, divided
  • Two carrots, cut into matchsticks
  • One clove of garlic, minced
  • One pinch of red pepper flakes
  • One pound of thinly-sliced beef top round steak
  • Four large eggs
  • Four cups of cooked white rice
  • Four teaspoons of seasame oil, divided
  • One teaspoon of sesame seeds
  • Two teaspoons of gochujang (Korean pepper paste), divided (Optional)

Steps

  1. Stir together cucumber pieces and gochujang paste in a bowl; set aside.
  2. Bring about 2 cups water to a boil in a large nonstick skillet and stir in spinach; cook until bright green and witted, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Drain spinach and squeeze out as much moisture as possible; set spinach aside in a bowl and stir in soy sauce.
  4. Heat 1 teaspoon of olive oil in a large nonstick skillet; cook and stir carrots until softened, about 3 minutes.
  5. Stir in garlic and cook just until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir in cucumber mixture; sprinkle with red pepper flakes. Set carrot mixture aside in a bowl.
  6. Brown beed in a clean nonstick skillet over medium heat, about 5 minutes per side; set side.
  7. Heat remaining one teaspoon of olive loil in another nonstick skillet over medium-low heat. Fry eggs just on one side until yolks are runny, but whites are fir, 2 to 4 minutes.
  8. Divide cooked rice into 4 large serving bowls; top with spinach mixture, a few pieces of beef, and cucumber mixture. Place 1 egg each serving. Drizzle each bowl with one teaspoon sesame oil, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and a small amount of gochujang paste if desired.
